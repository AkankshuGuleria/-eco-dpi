# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Eco-DPI is for evaluators, classmates, civic-tech reviewers, and student demo audiences who need to quickly understand a Digital Public Infrastructure simulation for environmental and municipal issue reporting. The imagined end users are citizens reporting issues and administrators monitoring incident clusters.

## Product Purpose

The product demonstrates a high-quality academic MVP where citizens submit location-aware civic reports, the backend deduplicates nearby similar reports, and administrators explore clustered incidents and heatmap intensity across Chandigarh.

## Positioning

Eco-DPI makes civic issue reporting feel like living environmental infrastructure: fast for citizens, spatially intelligent for administrators, and credible enough for a public-sector prototype.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: submit a sample report; preview the dashboard.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: civic reports become location intelligence.
- Belief ladder: the project captures real browser location, stores citizen reports, merges nearby duplicates, and turns incidents into map and heatmap insight.
- Proof on hand: the MVP scope includes React, browser geolocation, OpenStreetMap/Leaflet, local uploads, seeded Chandigarh incidents, and spatial deduplication.

## Brand Personality

The brand should feel civic, ecological, and kinetic. It should avoid both dry government-portal styling and generic climate-tech softness.

## Anti-references

Avoid beige eco brochures, generic leaf logos, purple AI gradients, dull municipal tables, and landing pages made from repeated feature cards.

## Design Principles

Show the system working before explaining it.

Make location intelligence visible through map-like layers, clusters, and motion.

Keep environmental cues physical: rain, greenery, public space, and civic infrastructure.

Balance bold presentation with public-service accessibility.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The web page should support keyboard navigation, strong color contrast, semantic structure, useful image alt text, visible focus states, and reduced-motion preferences.
